About

I'm an Applied Scientist at Amazon's Worldwide Buyer Risk Prevention team, where I build machine-learning systems that protect checkout and stop fraud and bad actors at scale. My work blends temporal graph learning over customer–device–payment interactions, knowledge graphs for behavioral profiling, transformer models for tabular and anomaly signals, and agentic LLM systems for automation — including a reinforcement-learning (GRPO) agent that adversarially red-teams production fraud-decisioning systems to surface evasion paths before they're exploited.

Before Amazon I earned my M.S. in Computer Science at Georgia Tech, interned on autonomous-driving perception at Aeva, and was a research assistant at Carnegie Mellon's Synergy Labs. Feel free to reach out to connect.
